The Band

John Lennon

Growing up in the middle-class village of Woolton on the outskirts of Liverpool everybody knew John Winston Lennon was different. Nobody could have guessed however that the local troublemaker would go on to change the world!

From Teddy Boy to avante-garde protester for peace. We follow John Lennon from his troubled up-bringing in Merseyside, through Beatlemania, married life in suburbia, the Indica Gallery meeting that would change him forever and to New York where he fought to live and eventually died.

For the ultimate Lennon fan we recommend our “New York, New York” package.

Paul McCartney

Born to a mid-wife and a salesman in 1942 Paul McCartney was the third Beatle to be born in Liverpool. The band’s bassist and half of the “Lennon/McCartney" song factory, James Paul McCartney went on to become the most successful artist the world has ever seen.

Join us as we take you to his house in Forthlin Road where as teenagers him & his mate John wrote “Please Please Me”, to Beatle Paul’s swinging London of nightclubs, theatres & his St. John’s Wood townhouse and back to basics at his Rye farmhouse where he & Linda raised their family.

All of our tours include important McCartney locations but to tick off all of the must-see Paul landmarks we recommend our Liverpool & Beyond Package for true Paul fans.

George Harrison

The youngest of 4 children, George started out life in an understated 'two-up-two-down' in Liverpool’s Wavertree. Training as an electrician, he decided to throw it all in for a rock'n'roll band expecting to just make a few bob. Fast forward two years and that band were conquering America!

Important George sites included in our tours take you from his first house at 12 Arnold Grove, Liverpool, to the bachelor flat he & Ringo shared in London, married life with Pattie Boyd in Esher, the gothic rock star mansion in Henley-on-Thames & his spiritual home of India!

Any of our packages will appeal to the Harrison fan but we especially recommend the Rishikesh - Chants of India Package if you really want to get a feel for the 'Quiet One'.

Ringo Starr

For rags-to-riches stories, Ringo’s has to be up there with the greats. Born Richard Starkey his upbringing on one of Liverpool’s harshest housing estates meant his childhood was tough. Ringo was a poorly child and spent most of his time in hospital, missing out on a decent education.

During one of his long stints in hospital he was handed a set of drumsticks. These would be the tools to get him out of the bleak life that seemed set out in front of him. He was a late comer to the band but from the moment Ringo stepped behind the drums for the Beatles the slow but steady success they had been experiencing suddenly sky rocketed. Once the 4 puzzle pieces were in place the world that Ringo knew changed beyond all recognition and he never looked back. Let us take you back to his tiny first home, the pub that his mother worked in at the bottom of his road and onwards and upwards through to central London townhouse and his mansions in England’s most prestigious towns. A remarkable story of the lad from the Dingle.

There is something for Ringo fans in all our tours however to really understand Ringo’s story we recommend our Liverpool package.

Liverpool Tour

Day 1

Our day starts with a morning of sightseeing on our psychedelic tour bus. Coach based with stops for photo opportunities at all of the points of interest:

• Menlove Avenue (John Lennon’s childhood home).

• St Peter’s Church Hall (Paul and John’s first meeting and where Paul auditioned for the Quarry Men).

• Elenor Rigby’s Grave.

• Forthlin Road (Paul’s childhood home).

• Strawberry Fields.

There is a break in the tour to go inside Strawberry Fields which is now open to the public. Opportunity to stop for refreshments.

• Penny Lane.

• Arnold Grove (George’s first House).

• Sefton Park (where John’s mum and dad met).

• Admiral Grove (Ringo’s childhood home and the pub which featured on his album cover for Sentimental Journey- please note we are unable to stop the bus here but there will be photo opportunities from the coach).

• LIPA - (now famous as the performing arts college funded by Paul McCartney but originally George and Paul’s secondary school the Liverpool Institute).

• The Philharmonic Dining Rooms (when John was famous he often said the thing he most missed was being able to pop into the Phil for a pint).

• We’ll also take you past primary schools, clubs and dancehalls they played in the early days and local hangouts as our tour guide paints a picture of the Beatles’ Liverpool through their eyes.

• Our last stop will be the Cavern Club and we’ll be just in time for the same lunchtime session that the boys played 292 times.

Free time to enjoy lunch and explore the Cavern Quarter either at leisure or with our ever ready tour guide.

For the beginning of the afternoon we stay within Matthew Street visiting the Beatle’s favourite watering hole post Cavern session The Grapes and then onto the Magical Mystery Museum, home to the biggest collection of Beatles memorabilia in the world.

From there our tour moves on foot to pass by the original site of the Liverpool Odeon where the premier of A Hard Day’s Night took place then pass Lime Street Station to the Adelphi Hotel (home to the annual Beatleweek Convention) to board our coach to move out of the city to West Derby.

• The Casbah - West Derby.

• Everyone has heard of the Cavern but the Beatles themselves argue that the Casbah was where it all really started.

• A home-made club in the basement of Pete Best’s family home has been astonishingly preserved. Your tour of this 1950’s coffee club will be conducted by Pete Best’s brother Roag (also owner of the Magical Mystery Museum), an insider who passionately shares his family’s story. Be prepared to see (and touch) original artwork by John, Paul and George and hear heart-warming stories of how the Casbah shaped the makings of the band.

Free time in Liverpool before dinner in Blake’s Restaurant at The Hard Day’s Night Hotel.

Day 1 has only just begun! After dinner, we’ll head to Bar 4 for drinks (and maybe a cocktail or two). A resident musician will play acoustic sets to get you in the mood for our Day 1 grand finale!

Get ready to twist and shout!!! We’ll head back down into the Cavern Club just in time for Beatles Tribute Act (Made in Liverpool) to hit that famous stage.

Day 2

This is the closest we have to a time machine!

Straight from the living room where John and Paul wrote Please, Please Me we’ll take you back to the Albert Dock where you’ll have plenty of photography time at the Beatle’s Statues on Pier Wharf before walking to the Beatle Story Museum.

London, beyond Tour

Day 1

See the Swinging London itinerary. With dinner at Beso, Covent Garden (A favourite restaurant of the band during the 60’s, we’ll dine in the Private Beatles Dining Room)

Day 2

Board the bus bright and early in St. John’s Wood as we prepare for our journey to Esher, Surrey.

Esher - Kinfauns (George Harrison bought a bungalow here to share with his wife Pattie Boyd. They painted the exterior in psychedelic colours and lived there until the 70’s. Although the bungalow no longer exists there is a blue plaque on the residence that has replaced it)

On to Weybridge, Surrey

Weybridge -Sunny Heights (just 10 minutes down the road from George you’d have found Ringo’s first family home. Complete with its own pub, Ringo and his wife Maureen started their family in this very well to do neighbourhood and kept Sunny Heights right up to the 1980’s.

Weybridge - Kenwood (a short walk from Sunny Heights we come to the mock Tudor mansion that belonged to John and Cynthia Lennon. The house where most of the Sgt.Pepper album was written and where Cyn found Yoko Ono wearing nothing but a silk kimono!)

Back on the coach to Ascot, Berkshire

Ascot - Tittenhurst Park (John and Yoko bought this house from Peter Sellers, had it all painted white, lived in it, recorded Imagine in it and then sold it to Ringo. Ringo held onto this house until the late 1980’s when he sold it to Dubai’s ex president.

Lunch and Free Time in Ascot.

Onwards to our final destination of Henley-on-Thames.

Henley-on-Thames - Friar Park (George moved into Friar Park in 1970 when the estate was in disrepair. He personally oversaw the refurbishment of the quirky, gothic pile restoring it to its former glory. It’s vast gardens were tended to by George and include Japanese gardens, underground lagoons and a replica of the Matterhorn.

Henley-on-Thames - The Row Barge Inn. Time to check in to our lodgings for the night. The Row Barge is a stones throw from Friar Park’s gates and served as George’s local. George popped in so regularly for a pint that he has his own stool at the bar. The landlord will regal you with stories of late night lock-ins and private jamming sessions over dinner.

Day 3

A few hours of free time to explore the Royal village (home to the Henley Regatta) before boarding the coach back to London where the tour comes to an end.

Hamburg Tour

There was nothing glamorous about their time in Germany. They played grimy clubs full of gangsters, pimps and drunken sailors. The girls they were chatting to between sets were prostitutes and their room was an old store cupboard behind in a blue movie theatre. The arduous 9 hour sets gave them grit and tightened them up as a band. The tough audiences demanded they be exciting and raw. Mixing with the Hamburg art students who had become fans of the band made them aware of the importance of their ‘look’ and their pushed back teddy boy hair started to fall forward in preparation for the mop top do of the 60’s. When The Beatles returned to Liverpool from Hamburg they were dressed in leather, they had edge and suddenly lots of people began to notice them.

With our Hamburg tour we visit the St.Pauli district. Famous for its bright lights and partying we’ll show you round the former red light district sharing stories of the Beatle’s time there as we stop by all of the major points of interest which include:

• Grosse Freheit (the main strip that the Beatles lived and played on)

• Indra Club (the first club that the band played at)

• The Bambi Kino (the cinema where the Beatles were given a store room to sleep in)

• The Kaiserkellar (after a 3 month residency at the Indra the Beatles were promoted to the Kaiserkellar. A bigger club but the conditions were just as poor as the previous gig. This is also where Rory Storm and the Hurricanes were already the resident band. Ringo Starr was the glamorous drummer for the Hurricanes, often sat in for Pete Best when he went awol, making this the first place that all four of the Beatles played together)

• Hamburg’s Seaman’s Mission (Ringo and the other Hurricanes used this as their digs)

• The Reeperbahn

• Top 10 Club (at the time this was the club all the bands wanted to play at. The Beatles moved to the Top 10 in October 1960. Paul became the band’s bassist during their time playing this club)

• The Star Club ("the toppermost of the poppermost!" Returning to Hamburg in April 1962, after a stint back in Liverpool, the Beatles were booked by the Star Club. A 2000 capacity club with better conditions. In Reeperbahn terms they had made it. They would come back for a 2nd residency in November 62 and this time they returned with Ringo as their drummer)

• Hotel Germania (the band’s living conditions also improved once they were booked at the Star Club. Hotel Germania became their home during their stint in early 1962)

Hotel Pacific (returning towards the end of 62 the Beatles were promoted to the Hotel Pacific. The beds behind a blue movie screen were becoming a distant memory)

New York Tour

New York became a defining moment in the Beatles’ story. It also looms large in John’s legend. It became his home, he fought to stay there and did until the day he died.

Our tour will walk you through those heady days of January 1964 when the 4 boys from Liverpool really did begin to change the world and we’ll show you around Lennon’s City.

A one day tour by foot across the city including the following locations:

• Plaza Hotel (the Beatles took over an entire floor of this regal hotel in February 1964 and had to have a police cordon around the full perimeter of the building to stop the throng of screaming fans from besieging it. This was the Beatle’s first visit to the States).

• Ed Sullivan Theatre (the Beatles’s appearance on the Ed Sullivan Show was filmed at this theatre. 73 million people tuned in to watch the 4 mop tops that evening. This is still believed to be the largest audience for a television program to date).

• Madison Square Gardens (John’s last ever performance was here when he appeared on stage for a duet with Elton John. The Beatles also broke records in the same stadium).

• St Regis Hotel (This is the hotel that John and Yoko stayed in for their first few months in New York).

• The Hit Factory (John was a regular visitor to the recording studios and recorded most of his solo albums here).

• Smiths Bar (Just a stroll from the Hit Factory this burger joint was a favourite late night spot for John).

• Bank Street (John and Yoko’s first home in the City).

• The Dakota (The long term home for John and Yoko. This is also the place where John met his death. Yoko still lives here now).

• Central Park, Strawberry Fields (Yoko’s memorial to John).

Rishikesh Tour

In February 1968 the Beatles travelled to India to study transcendental meditation under the Maharishi Mahesh Yogi. Joined by a host of celebrity pals & reporters the world looked on bewildered.

Today the pictures and footage from that trip have become iconic. As Sgt. Pepper had turned a generation onto LSD the Beatles trip to India put that same generation on a different path.

The band were inspired creatively by their time at the ashram, writing some of their most iconic songs during their stay and for George Harrison it would be the start of a spiritual journey that he spent the rest of his life travelling.

Day 1 - The Beatle’s Ashram

The abandoned enclosure of bungalows and meditation domes no longer emit the luxury that they would have when the Beatles came to stay but the encroaching jungle and decaying shrines give an ambience of peace and tranquillity. The Maharishi’s decadent house still stands and the rooms that the Beatles slept and meditated in are open for visitors to peruse at their leisure. We’ll spend the day exploring this fascinating site. Locals and travellers alike gather daily with acoustic guitars strumming and singing the songs that the Beatles wrote during their time adding to the ambience of a time gone by.

Day 2 - The Shiva Temple

A day tour through the Himalayas to this amazing temple. Stopping en route to see the hand washing ritual on the banks of the River Ganges we’ll take the same route that the Beatles took when they took a tour with the Maharishi.

Days 3-4 - Yoga and Meditation Retreat

This beautiful hideaway in the holy Himalayas is built around the same teachings of the Maharishi that the Beatles were so eager to learn about back in 1968. This is the time to step out of modern life and immerse yourself in the simplicity of basic living. The ashram has a easy to follow programme of meditation and yoga as well as sitar lessons for those who really want to throw themselves into the George experience. All meals included.

This tour runs from February- April only.
