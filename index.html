The Band

John Lennon

Growing up in the middle-class village of Woolton on the outskirts of Liverpool everybody knew John Winston Lennon was different. Nobody could have guessed however that the local troublemaker would go on to change the world! For the ultimate Lennon fan we recommend our “New York, New York” package.

Paul McCartney

Born to a mid-wife & a salesman in 1942 Paul McCartney was the third Beatle to be born in Liverpool. The band’s bassist & half of the “Lennon/McCartney song factory, James Paul McCartney went on to become the most successful solo artist the world has ever seen. All of our tours include important McCartney locations but to tick off all of the must-see Paul landmarks we recommend our Liverpool & Beyond Package for true Paul fans

George Harrison

The youngest of 4 children, George started out life in an understated 2 up 2 down in Liverpool’s Wavertree. He was training as an electrician when he decided to throw it all in for a rock n roll band expecting to just make a few bob. Fast forward 2 years & that rock n roll band were conquering America! Any of our packages will appeal to the Harrison fan but we especially recommend the Rishikesh - Chants of India Package if you really want to get a feel for the Quiet One!

Packages

Liverpool

Birthplace of the Beatles

Let me take you down because I’m going to... Strawberry Fields....

Arguably the most nostalgic and atmospheric of our packages and a must for any Beatle fan. Let us take you back to where it all began. Penny Lane, the Cavern, their childhood homes. If the boys breathed near it we can guarantee we’ll visit it. A two day tour which follows the Beatles from birth to the brink of superstardom and beyond in a city that holds its sons forever in its heart.

Liverpool Tour

Day 1

Our day starts with a morning of sightseeing on our psychedelic tour bus. Coach based with stops for photo opportunities at all of the points of interest:

• Menlove Avenue (John Lennon’s childhood home).

• St Peter’s Church Hall (Paul and John’s first meeting and where Paul auditioned for the Quarry Men).

• Elenor Rigby’s Grave.

• Forthlin Road (Paul’s childhood home).

• Strawberry Fields.

There is a break in the tour to go inside Strawberry Fields which is now open to the public. Opportunity to stop for refreshments.

• Penny Lane.

• Arnold Grove (George’s first House).

• Sefton Park (where John’s mum and dad met).

• Admiral Grove (Ringo’s childhood home and the pub which featured on his album cover for Sentimental Journey- please note we are unable to stop the bus here but there will be photo opportunities from the coach).

• LIPA - (now famous as the performing arts college funded by Paul McCartney but originally George and Paul’s secondary school the Liverpool Institute).

• The Philharmonic Dining Rooms (when John was famous he often said the thing he most missed was being able to pop into the Phil for a pint).

• We’ll also take you past primary schools, clubs and dancehalls they played in the early days and local hangouts as our tour guide paints a picture of the Beatles’ Liverpool through their eyes.

• Our last stop will be the Cavern Club and we’ll be just in time for the same lunchtime session that the boys played 292 times.

Free time to enjoy lunch and explore the Cavern Quarter either at leisure or with our ever ready tour guide.

For the beginning of the afternoon we stay within Matthew Street visiting the Beatle’s favourite watering hole post Cavern session The Grapes and then onto the Magical Mystery Museum, home to the biggest collection of Beatles memorabilia in the world.

From there our tour moves on foot to pass by the original site of the Liverpool Odeon where the premier of A Hard Day’s Night took place then pass Lime Street Station to the Adelphi Hotel (home to the annual Beatleweek Convention) to board our coach to move out of the city to West Derby.

• The Casbah - West Derby.

• Everyone has heard of the Cavern but the Beatles themselves argue that the Casbah was where it all really started.

• A home-made club in the basement of Pete Best’s family home has been astonishingly preserved. Your tour of this 1950’s coffee club will be conducted by Pete Best’s brother Roag (also owner of the Magical Mystery Museum), an insider who passionately shares his family’s story. Be prepared to see (and touch) original artwork by John, Paul and George and hear heart-warming stories of how the Casbah shaped the makings of the band.

Free time in Liverpool before dinner in Blake’s Restaurant at The Hard Day’s Night Hotel.

Day 1 has only just begun! After dinner, we’ll head to Bar 4 for drinks (and maybe a cocktail or two). A resident musician will play acoustic sets to get you in the mood for our Day 1 grand finale!

Get ready to twist and shout!!! We’ll head back down into the Cavern Club just in time for Beatles Tribute Act (Made in Liverpool) to hit that famous stage.

Day 2

Rise and Shine!

We’ll meet you at the Jury’s Inn by the Albert Dock to whisk you back in time. We’ll revisit Menlove Avenue and Forthlin Road but this time we’ll go inside. Each property has its own custodian who literally lives and breathes their houses. The childhood homes have been lovingly restored by the National Trust with original and replica furnishings. This is the closest we have to a time machine!

Straight from the living room where John and Paul wrote Please, Please Me we’ll take you back to the Albert Dock where you’ll have plenty of photography time at the Beatle’s Statues on Pier Wharf before walking to the Beatle Story Museum.

Free time and lunch on the Albert Dock.

Walking Tour of L1

John was at Art College, Paul and George next door at the Liverpool Institute, there was something in the air, they felt something better was coming, an exciting time which we’ll lead through on foot, tracing the footsteps of the Beatles just before the world took them away from Liverpool! Tour Includes:

• The Philly.

• Gambier Terrace (John Lennon and Stuart Sutcliffe’s student flat).

• Falkener Street (Brian Epstein’s home which he gave to John and Cynthia when they first married).

• The Jacaranda.

• Blue Angel Club.

• NEMS.

• Ye Olde Crack.

• LIPA.

Evening ride on the Ferry across the Mersey.

We fell in love with this glorious city and the stories it tells we hope with this package we can share that magic with you!

London

Swinging London

How does it feel to be one of the beautiful people?

Let us take you back to 1960’s London, where the girls wore flowers in their hair and boys wore flowery shirts, mods rode their scooters and the Beatles presided over them all!!

London Tour

We’ll visit:

• The London Palladium (birthplace of Beatlemania)

• Carnaby Street

• Bag o Nail (Paul’s favourite nightspot and where he met Linda Eastman)

• Savile Row (Apple Corps former offices and home to the famous rooftop gig)

• MPL (Paul’s current offices)

• Wimpole Street (Jane Asher’s family home and the place that her then boyfriend, Paul McCartney lodged in the attic & wrote many of the Beatlemania hits)

• Marylebone Registry Office (Paul married his 1st and 3rd wives here)

• Montague Square (Ringo, John 'n' Yoko and Jimi Hendrix all lived here at some point. John and Yoko photographed the famous cover for their Two Virgins album here, they also got busted for drugs in this very flat)

• Baker Street (the former Apple Boutique residence)

• Green Street (the bachelor pad that all 4 Beatles lived in when they first moved down south)

• Indica Gallery (the hip art gallery where John Lennon 1st set eyes on Yoko Ono)

• This is where we’ll jump on the tube to St John’s Wood & take a stroll down to that famous crossing on Abbey Road.

• Cavendish Avenue (the townhouse Paul bought in the late 60’s & is still used as his London residence whenever he is in town)

After Liverpool, London is the place that still holds that 60’s spirit alive. The Beatles were the epitome of everything that London offered during this explosive era & we hope with our tour guides informative narrative this package will transport you back to a time where the young walked to a very different beat!

London, beyond!

London and beyond

Picture yourself on a boat on a river...

When the Beatles got bored of the bright lights of London they moved to the suburbs to very big houses in very pretty places. After a day of stepping back in time in the big city join us for a road trip out of town to where George, John & Ringo up sticks and called home.

London, beyond Tour

Day 1

See the Swinging London itinerary. With dinner at Beso, Covent Garden (A favourite restaurant of the band during the 60’s, we’ll dine in the Private Beatles Dining Room)

Day 2

Board the bus bright and early in St. John’s Wood as we prepare for our journey to Esher, Surrey.

Esher - Kinfauns (George Harrison bought a bungalow here to share with his wife Pattie Boyd. They painted the exterior in psychedelic colours and lived there until the 70’s. Although the bungalow no longer exists there is a blue plaque on the residence that has replaced it)

On to Weybridge, Surrey

Weybridge -Sunny Heights (just 10 minutes down the road from George you’d have found Ringo’s first family home. Complete with its own pub, Ringo and his wife Maureen started their family in this very well to do neighbourhood and kept Sunny Heights right up to the 1980’s.

Weybridge - Kenwood (a short walk from Sunny Heights we come to the mock Tudor mansion that belonged to John and Cynthia Lennon. The house where most of the Sgt.Pepper album was written and where Cyn found Yoko Ono wearing nothing but a silk kimono!)

Back on the coach to Ascot, Berkshire

Ascot - Tittenhurst Park (John and Yoko bought this house from Peter Sellers, had it all painted white, lived in it, recorded Imagine in it and then sold it to Ringo. Ringo held onto this house until the late 1980’s when he sold it to Dubai’s ex president.

Lunch and Free Time in Ascot.

Onwards to our final destination of Henley-on-Thames.

Henley-on-Thames - Friar Park (George moved into Friar Park in 1970 when the estate was in disrepair. He personally oversaw the refurbishment of the quirky, gothic pile restoring it to its former glory. It’s vast gardens were tended to by George and include Japanese gardens, underground lagoons and a replica of the Matterhorn.

Henley-on-Thames - The Row Barge Inn. Time to check in to our lodgings for the night. The Row Barge is a stones throw from Friar Park’s gates and served as George’s local. George popped in so regularly for a pint that he has his own stool at the bar. The landlord will regal you with stories of late night lock-ins and private jamming sessions over dinner.

Day 3

A few hours of free time to explore the Royal village (home to the Henley Regatta) before boarding the coach back to London where the tour comes to an end.
